# QuantEcon Style Audit — Cross-Series Report

- **Audit date:** 2026-05-27
- **Scope:** 5 lecture series, **292 lectures** audited against the [QuantEcon style guide](https://github.com/QuantEcon/QuantEcon.manual/tree/main/manual/styleguide/) (`writing.md` + `math.md`).
- **Scoring dimensions:** Writing style (rules W1–W8) and Math conventions (rules M1–M14). See [`_AUDIT_SPEC.md`](_AUDIT_SPEC.md) for the canonical rubric and report template that every series subagent used.

## About this repository

This repo follows the QuantEcon **`audit.YYYY-MM.{topic}`** convention for time-bounded audits:

- The date prefix indicates when the audit was performed — useful for assessing staleness at a glance.
- The topic suffix (`style-guide` here) identifies what was audited.
- These repos are **disposable**: once an audit's findings have been actioned (or its data has been superseded by a fresher audit), the repo can be archived or deleted without affecting other QuantEcon infrastructure.

**What's committed here:**

- [`_AUDIT_SPEC.md`](_AUDIT_SPEC.md) — the canonical scoring rubric and report template (v2, aligned with [`QuantEcon/action-style-guide`](https://github.com/QuantEcon/action-style-guide) `qe-*` rule IDs)
- This top-level `README.md` — cross-series synthesis with corpus-wide patterns and prioritisation
- Five series-level `README.md` files — one per audited lecture series, with averages, priority distribution, top systemic issues, and per-lecture ranking tables

**What's not committed:**

- The 292 per-lecture reports produced during the audit. They were generated by parallel subagents under spec v1 with some inter-subagent calibration drift; the series-level synthesis is the durable artifact. The per-lecture reports are preserved in the audit author's working tree for evidence and can be re-published if needed.

## Cross-series systemic patterns

Issues are ranked by total lecture-count across all five series. Counts come from the per-series READMEs.

### 1. **W3 — Title Case in section/subsection headings** (≈170+ / 292 lectures)
**The single dominant violation in the corpus.** Top issue in every series.

- `lecture-python.myst`: 87 / 110 (79 %)
- `lecture-python-programming`: 24 / 26 (92 %)
- `lecture-dp`: 21 / 50 (42 %)
- `lecture-python-intro`: 19 / 51 (37 %)
- `lecture-python-advanced.myst`: low (subheading case is mostly correct here)

H2/H3/H4 headings should use sentence case (capitalize only the first word + proper nouns). A single scripted pass — preserving acronyms (LQ, IID, SVD, OLG, OLS, AR1, GE, McCall, Bellman, Markov, Stackelberg, etc.) — would lift the writing score across most of the corpus.

### 2. **M2 — Transpose with `'`, `^T`, or `^\prime` instead of `^\top`** (~46 lectures)
- `lecture-python-advanced.myst`: 15 / 55
- `lecture-python.myst`: 17 / 110
- `lecture-dp`: 11 / 50
- `lecture-python-intro`: 3 / 51

Concentrated in the older LQ / Riccati / linear-algebra lectures: `lqcontrol`, `linear_algebra`, `lagrangian_lqdp`, `perm_income_cons`, `markov_perf`, `cross_product_trick`, `robustness`, `classical_filtering`, `black_litterman`, `smoothing`, `tax_smoothing_*`. Careful sweep needed to preserve derivative-prime (`u'`, `v'`) and next-period notation (`x'`, `\theta'`).

### 3. **M7 — Bare `E`, `E_t`, `\Pr`, `\Var` instead of `\mathbb{E}` / `\mathbb{P}` / `\mathbb{V}`** (~60 lectures)
- `lecture-python.myst`: 21 / 110
- `lecture-python-advanced.myst`: 17 / 55
- `lecture-dp`: 17 / 50
- `lecture-python-programming`: 5 / 26
- `lecture-python-intro`: minor

Affects expectation-heavy lectures across all series. Includes the missing-braces form `\mathbb E_t` (vs the required `\mathbb{E}_t`).

### 4. **M9 — `\mathcal{N}` (or `{\cal N}`) for the normal distribution instead of plain `N`** (~30 lectures)
- `lecture-python-advanced.myst`: 14 / 55
- `lecture-dp`: 9 / 50
- `lecture-python.myst`: 6 / 110

Mechanical fix. Multi-letter distribution names should switch to `\mathrm{Beta}`, `\mathrm{Bernoulli}`, etc.

### 5. **M4 — Matrices using `pmatrix`/`matrix`/`array` instead of `bmatrix`** (~24 lectures, heavy in a few)
- `lecture-python-advanced.myst`: 14 / 55 (DLE-class lectures: `growth_in_dles`, `cattle_cycles`, `lucas_asset_pricing_dles`, `permanent_income_dles`, `hs_invertibility_example`)
- `lecture-dp`: 5 / 50 (but `lqcontrol` alone has 17 array matrices; `lq_inventories` has 11)
- Smaller counts in the other series

Highest-impact targets concentrate in a small number of math-heavy files.

### 6. **M5 — `\mathbf` / `\boldsymbol` used for vectors/matrices** (~27 lectures)
- `lecture-python-advanced.myst`: 3 / 55 (but heavy within those files)
- `lecture-python.myst`: 9 / 110
- `lecture-dp`: 15 / 50 (incl. `\vec`, `\mathscr`, `\mathsf`, `\cal`)

The style guide forbids bold for vectors/matrices.

### 7. **W6 — "i.i.d." or "iid" instead of "IID"** (~30 lectures)
- `lecture-python.myst`: 21 / 110
- `lecture-python-advanced.myst`: 5 / 55
- A handful in other series

Trivial global-replace — write "IID" in narrative text.

### 8. **W1 — Multi-sentence paragraphs** (~100 lectures, mostly Low severity)
- `lecture-python-intro`: 47 / 51 (mostly Overview sections)
- `lecture-dp`: 43 / 50 (Low severity)
- `lecture-python-programming`: 8 / 26

Pervasive but typically low-severity. The style guide asks for one-sentence paragraphs. A targeted pass on Overview sections would yield the biggest readability gains.

### 9. **M3 — `\mathbf{1}` for ones/indicator instead of `\mathbb{1}`** (~13 lectures)
- `lecture-python.myst`: 9 / 110
- `lecture-python-intro`: 4 / 51
- Small counts elsewhere

### 10. **W7 — Inconsistent quotes / random mid-sentence capitalization** (~50 lectures)
- `lecture-python-intro`: 23 / 51
- `lecture-dp`: 20 / 50 (smart quotes)
- Smaller counts elsewhere

TeX-style ``\`\`...''`` quotes, mixed smart/straight quotes, mid-sentence Title Case for non-proper-nouns.

## Recommended remediation order

A high-leverage way to lift scores across the entire corpus, ordered by impact / effort:

### Pass 1 — Mechanical sweeps (cheap, very high impact)
1. **Section-heading case (W3)** — script that lowercases all H2/H3/H4 first words except proper nouns / acronyms. Targets ~170 lectures.
2. **`IID` normalization (W6)** — global replace `i.i.d.` / `iid` → `IID` in narrative. ~30 lectures.
3. **`\mathcal{N}` → `N` (M9)** — distribution-naming sweep. ~30 lectures.
4. **`\mathbb E` → `\mathbb{E}` (M7, braces only)** — add braces to all blackboard operators where missing. Easy regex.

### Pass 2 — Targeted file edits (moderate effort)
5. **Build-risk fixes** — fix `divergence_measures.md:134` (`align` → `aligned`) and `cross_product_trick.md:133` (broken `{eq}` ref) immediately.
6. **Transpose convention (M2)** — sweep prime / `^T` → `^\top` in the LQ-family cluster (`lqcontrol`, `lagrangian_lqdp`, `perm_income_cons`, `cross_product_trick`, `markov_perf`, `robustness`, `classical_filtering`, `black_litterman`, `smoothing`, `tax_smoothing_*`). **Careful** to preserve derivative-prime (`u'`, `v'`) and next-period notation (`x'`).
7. **`E_t` → `\mathbb{E}_t` (M7)** — bare-expectation sweep on the same cluster + Ramsey / IFP / smoothing lectures.

### Pass 3 — Bigger refactors (focus on HIGH-priority list)
8. **Matrix environments (M4)** — convert `array`/`pmatrix`/`matrix` to `bmatrix` in `lqcontrol`, `lq_inventories`, `lagrangian_lqdp`, DLE-class lectures.
9. **Vector decoration (M5)** — strip `\mathbf` / `\boldsymbol` / `\vec` / `\mathscr` from vectors in `hs_recursive_models`, `robustness`, the `calvo*` lectures, `dyn_stack`.
10. **Restructure `supply_demand_foundations_v2.md`** — give it a single Title Case H1 at top, fix typos.

### Pass 4 — Editorial (high effort, smaller per-lecture impact)
11. **One-sentence paragraphs (W1)** — manual editing pass on the Overview sections of every lecture (script-assisted but human-driven).

## Notes on calibration

- Scoring was deliberately strict: a 9–10 score required essentially no deviations. Most QuantEcon lectures have at least minor issues, so most scores cluster in the 7–9 range.
- The Math dimension was scored only for lectures containing math. Lectures with no math (e.g. `intro.md`, `troubleshooting.md`, `status.md`, `zreferences.md`, historical / data lectures like `business_cycle.md`, `french_rev.md`, `inflation_history.md`) were graded on Writing alone, and that score becomes the overall.
- Series-level subagents worked in parallel from the same `_AUDIT_SPEC.md`. Cross-series consistency was verified at the rollup stage — common rule IDs and identical severity definitions were applied everywhere.
- This audit covers **writing.md** and **math.md** only, as requested. The QuantEcon style guide also contains `code.md`, `figures.md`, `admonitions.md`, `references.md`, `doclinks.md`, `repos.md`, and `jax-conversion.md`. A follow-up audit covering those would be worthwhile, particularly for code-style conformance and figure-caption conventions.
